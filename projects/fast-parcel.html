Fast Parcel

Before the tariff situation between India and the USA, I was interested (and still am) in building a shipping and logistics service focused on Indian exporters. The idea came from working closely with existing logistics providers and seeing where the systems break down in practice.

I’ve spent more than four years in the logistics sector, dealing with pricing, fulfillment, coordination, and the realities of operating time-sensitive systems. At some point, relying entirely on current providers felt limiting, so I decided to explore what it would take to build something of my own. A big move, especially with zero software background.

Since I wasn’t a developer, I leaned heavily on AI to get started. With its help, I built a simple marketing site for Fast Parcel and hosted it using Cloudflare Pages. That part worked. Building the actual application did not.

I tried and failed to create the full system using AI alone. That failure made one thing clear: without understanding fundamentals, tools can only take you so far. If you don’t know how things work underneath, neither AI nor anyone else can help you build something reliable.

That experience became my motivation to start learning programming seriously, with a focus on understanding systems rather than just assembling features.
